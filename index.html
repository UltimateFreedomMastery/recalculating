Recalculating

On knowing where you stand

Read

Life, for many, is like trying to get to your favorite restaurant using a GPS that doesn't know where you are. You trust it — why wouldn't you? — and you follow every instruction.

"Turn left." You turn left. "Continue straight." You continue straight.

At first, nothing seems wrong. You're moving. You're progressing. The map updates. The voice stays confident.

But then a subtle tension creeps in. You notice the scenery doesn't quite match what you expected. The turns feel slightly off. The timing feels strange — too early, too late.

You assume the problem can't be the GPS. It's authoritative. It sounds certain. Everyone else is using one too. So you double down. You follow the directions more carefully. You stop questioning. You override your instincts when they whisper, "This feels wrong."

Along the way, you find places that are decent. Sometimes even really good. Good food. Good company. Good moments. Enough to explain why you're not unhappy. Enough to justify staying longer than you planned.

But never quite enough to quiet the feeling that something is missing.

Occasionally, you wonder if you made a wrong turn somewhere — but the GPS never says you did. It just recalculates.

And recalculates. And recalculates.

You're always "five minutes away." Always "almost there." Always one more turn from arrival.

Years pass like this. From the outside, it looks like you're doing everything right. You followed the instructions. You stayed on the road. You didn't give up.

Yet somehow… you never arrive.

Not because the destination was unrealistic. Not because you lacked discipline, intelligence, or effort. But because the system guiding you never had your true location to begin with.

When you don't know where you're standing, every direction becomes suspect — no matter how precise it sounds. No amount of optimization can fix a mislocated starting point. No better route can compensate for a false orientation.

And so life becomes a series of near-misses.

Almosts Good-enoughs Temporary satisfactions

That quietly exhaust you.

Until one day, a different question arises — not "What's the right direction?" but: "Wait… where am I actually starting from?"

That question changes everything. Because the moment your true location is known,
the destination was never as far away as it seemed.
